Mount Miniscule

The towers above the snowy range, blanketed in eternal ice. Officially, it’s named after Sir George Miniscule the famous explorer who, curiously, never climbed it.

Why? According to legend, Sir George confessed in private: “I don’t want to be eaten by aliens.” He claimed he’d seen glowing lights in the sky near the mountain and heard strange voices carried on the wind. Terrified, he refused to set foot on its slopes.

The Expedition Council mocked him, calling the peak “Miniscule” to downplay his warnings. But his journals vanished, and his fears… well, they might not have been so foolish after all.

Travelers today still whisper of green flares in the night and machines humming beneath the ice. Some believe an ancient starship lies frozen below, its alien guardians waiting patiently for the next curious climber.

Maybe Sir George wasn’t cowardly — maybe he was the wisest of us all.
